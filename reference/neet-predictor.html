NEET Counseling Process

From rank declaration to college allotment — understand every step.

STEP 01 📋

MCC AIQ Registration

Register on mcc.nic.in for 15% All India Quota seats in government colleges and 100% seats in central/deemed institutions.

STEP 02 🏛️

State Counseling

Register with your state DME for 85% state quota seats. Each state has its own schedule — Delhi, MH, TN, UP run independently.

STEP 03 ✅

Choice Filling

Fill and lock your college-course preferences in order of priority. More choices = better chances. Don't leave slots empty.

STEP 04 🔄

Multiple Rounds

AIQ runs 3 rounds + stray vacancy. States run 2–5 rounds. Mop-up rounds fill remaining seats. Each round matters.

STEP 05 📄

Document Verification

Marksheets, category certificates, domicile proof, ID, photos. Prepare all originals + 5 sets of photocopies before reporting.

STEP 06 🎓

Reporting & Admission

Report to allotted college within the deadline. Pay fee, submit documents. Miss the deadline and you lose the seat.

// counseling types
All Quota Types Explained

15% All India Quota (AIQ)

MCC conducts this for all govt medical colleges except J&K. Rank-based, category seats reserved. AIIMS & JIPMER 100% through AIQ.

85% State Quota

State DME conducts for domicile holders. Separate rank lists per state. Often has OBC-NCL/EWS requirements specific to state.

Deemed Universities

Private deemed like Manipal, JSSMC, Amrita, Sri Ramachandra. 100% seats through MCC. Fees ₹10–25L/yr. No state quota.

Private College (State)

Management + NRI quota (15%+). NRI quota fees up to ₹25L/yr. Management quota through state authority. Some via NEET score directly.

AIIMS (13 Institutes)

Top 50 rank AIR for AIIMS Delhi. Other AIIMS accessible till rank ~2000 (UR). SC/ST/OBC cutoffs significantly lower. Best PG prospects.

ESIC / Armed Forces / AFMC

ESIC medical colleges via AIQ counseling. AFMC Pune has separate eligibility including SSB interview. Armed Forces quota for wards.

Category Reservation

  • UR: No reservation benefit
  • OBC-NCL: 27% (central quota)
  • SC: 15% | ST: 7.5%
  • EWS: 10% (central only)
  • PH: 5% horizontal in all
  • State OBC lists may differ

Common Questions
The qualifying percentile is 50th for UR (720–164 score range). For OBC/SC/ST it is 40th percentile (163–129). For PH it is 45th percentile (163–146). However, for actual college admissions, cutoffs vary widely by college and are much higher.
Yes, absolutely. AIQ and state counseling run simultaneously. You can register and participate in both. Getting a seat in AIQ does not bar you from state counseling and vice versa — but you’ll need to resign from one if you get allotted seats in both.
Class 10 certificate (DOB proof), Class 12 marksheet & certificate, NEET admit card & scorecard, ID proof (Aadhaar/Passport), passport photos, category certificate (SC/ST/OBC/EWS — central/state as applicable), domicile certificate for state quota, PwD certificate if applicable.
MCC AIQ runs 3 main rounds: Round 1, Round 2, and a Mop-Up Round. After the mop-up, Stray Vacancy rounds fill remaining seats. You can upgrade or change choices between rounds.
Yes — very important distinction. For AIQ/central institutions, you need a Central OBC-NCL certificate. Many state OBC certificates are not valid for AIQ. Check the MCC website for the exact certificate format required.
EWS (Economically Weaker Sections) provides 10% reservation in central/AIQ institutions for those not covered under SC/ST/OBC with family income below ₹8 lakh/year. You need an EWS certificate from a competent authority (Tehsildar/SDM level).
Deemed universities have no state quota — all seats go through MCC AIQ counseling. Fees range ₹8–25 lakh/year with no government subsidy. NRI quota (15% seats) can cost up to ₹40–60 lakh/year. Always check with the individual university’s fee committee.
Yes, in subsequent rounds you can upgrade to a better college if your rank allows. If you accept an upgraded seat, the earlier seat is automatically cancelled. In the final (Mop-Up) round, allotments are generally binding.
Missing the reporting deadline means forfeiture of the allotted seat. You may still participate in subsequent rounds if available. Always keep buffer time — reporting deadlines are strict and generally non-extendable.
AFMC Pune selects based on NEET rank + SSB interview + medical fitness (military standards). You must apply separately to AFMC after NEET results. Seats are funded by the government but bond service in the Armed Forces is required after MBBS.
